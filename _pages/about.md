I am a research staff assistant in the [Salzman Lab](https://zuckermaninstitute.columbia.edu/) at Columbia University's Zuckerman Institute, where I work with Dr. Evan Kyzar on the neurobiology of alcohol use disorder.

My work asks which brain regions change as voluntary alcohol drinking escalates, and which change again during withdrawal. Rather than recording one region at a time, we use a two-timepoint activity labeling method that tags the neurons active at two separate moments within the same brain, then reconstruct and count cells across the entire brain to find regions nobody has thought to look at. Day to day that means immunohistochemistry, confocal microscopy, chemogenetics, and behavioral assays including conditioned place preference, conditioned taste aversion, and alcohol preference, followed by a great deal of image analysis. I am currently leading a project testing whether GLP-1 receptor agonists reduce voluntary alcohol use and preference.

Before Columbia I worked in the Becher Lab at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai, investigating RBPJ-SIX1 as a tumor suppressor axis in diffuse intrinsic pontine glioma. I built mouse models of DIPG and characterized tumor growth using RNA extraction, Western blots, immunofluorescence, and MRI. As an undergraduate at Fordham I directed three independent projects in Dr. Eduardo Gallo's lab on ventral pallidum cholinergic projection neurons in cue-driven motivated behavior, using in vivo fiber photometry.

Alongside the bench, I am Co-Director of Research at Nucleate NY, where I lead scientific and commercial due diligence on early-stage life science ventures for the Activator program. I also mentor high school students through BioBus's Harlem Women in STEM Junior Scientist program at the Zuckerman Institute, and previously helped plan community neuroscience events with Columbia University Neuroscience Outreach.

I am completing a Master of Arts in Biotechnology at Columbia University, expected in October 2027.
